GETTING HELP WITH PYTHON – STEPS TO TAKE

Resources are in place to help you, but the first move is yours.

1. Did you prepare yourself? Have you read the textbook and worked through the learning resources and activities that cover the concepts and skills you need? You should complete all of these tasks before tackling the programming assignment.

1.    There is a Course Forum where you can ask questions about Python, or anything else about this course. Your professor, or a classmate, will respond to help you. Be sure to state the issue clearly.

2.    You can email your instructor with a Python question. For help with a program, attach it to the email in MyCourses. 

3.    You can seek out a free tutor at a campus Learning Support Center, but check for availability. Online help might be possible, too. Start your search here: http://www.spcollege.edu/tutoring/

4.    Your professor may have office hours. Refer to your professor’s instructor page at http://web.spcollege.edu/instructors/ but be sure to make an appointment. Professors are sometimes away at meetings.

5.   Browse to https://www.w3schools.com/python/  This excellent site covers everything and includes examples that run.

Above all, take action immediately to avoid falling behind! Do not procrastinate!
